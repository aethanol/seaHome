The Issues

Homelessness is an issue that affects everyone. Learn more about policy, causes of homelessness, structural barriers and social stigma.

History

Lean about the roots that first started the issue of people becoming affected by homelessness in the modern world, as well as why homelessness is a growing concern in our world.

Policy

Learn more about the policies that pertain to people affected by homelessness, such as policies on investments regarding permanent supportive housing and homeless veterans. Understanding policies about the people affected by homelessness will help to give you a greater idea of how you can help.

Advocacy

How to talk to your peers about the problems affecting the homeless and communicate with policymakers. Teach others about how programs in their community are preventing and ending homelessness in their city. Advocacy is the best way to cultivate the political support needed to get the resources needed to prevent and end homelessness.

Barriers

Learn about the many different obstacles and hardships that people affected by homelessness have to go through on a daily basis and understand the realities of people affected by homelessness

Solutions

Taking Action

Anyone can be part of the solution. Taking action can be as simple as donating time or money or becoming an advocate for people affected by homelessness.

Volunteering

Learn about how you can volunteer your time by connecting you with the local organizations that are dedicated to outreach for those who are affected by homelessness. Take action in your community and see how your time can help make tangible differences in the lives of those in need.

Donating

Information about how, when, and why to donate money in order to enact social change in your community. Learn about the pros and cons of giving cash to homeless and get connected with local resources that you can donate to.

How to talk to your peers about the problems affecting the homeless and communicate with policymakers. Advocacy is the best way to cultivate the political support needed to get the resources needed to prevent and end homelessness.

Lobbying

How to influence policy and the existing social systems and a way to create a system that is easier to navigate for those affected by homelessness. Ways to lobby elected officials like writing letters, meeting in person with, calling, or emailing. By connecting with and pushing for policy reform from elected officials and state legislators you can enact a greater change on the systemic problems facing those affected by homelessness than on your own.
